# Deep Work: Principles for Focused Output

**Deep work** is professional activity performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that pushes your cognitive capabilities to their limit.

## The Core Claim

The ability to do deep work is becoming increasingly rare and increasingly valuable. Those who cultivate it will thrive.

## The Four Depth Philosophies

| Philosophy | Schedule | Best For |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Monastic** | Deep work only, always | Writers, researchers |
| **Bimodal** | Alternating deep/shallow periods | Academics, consultants |
| **Rhythmic** | Fixed daily deep work block | Most knowledge workers |
| **Journalistic** | Opportunistic deep work | Experienced practitioners |

The **rhythmic** approach works for most people: protect a 2–4 hour block every morning before the day's interruptions begin.

## Practical Rules

1. **Work deeply**: Ritualize the start of deep work sessions. Same time, same place.
2. **Embrace boredom**: Practice resisting the urge to check your phone in idle moments. This trains focus.
3. **Quit social media**: Evaluate each tool by whether its benefits outweigh its costs — not by whether it has *any* benefit.
4. **Drain the shallows**: Time-block your entire day. Schedule shallow work so it can't bleed into deep time.

## The Metric

Track your deep work hours weekly. Most knowledge workers average fewer than 1 hour of genuine deep work per day. The theoretical maximum is around 4 hours before diminishing returns set in.
