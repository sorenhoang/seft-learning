# Visual Hierarchy: The Designer's Most Powerful Tool

Visual hierarchy is the arrangement of elements to show their order of importance. It answers the question: *where should the user look first?*

## The Six Tools of Hierarchy

### 1. Size
Larger = more important. Your primary heading should be noticeably larger than secondary text.

### 2. Weight
**Bold text** draws the eye even at the same size as surrounding text.

### 3. Color and Contrast
High contrast = high importance. Use muted colors for supporting information.

### 4. Spacing
Whitespace is not empty — it's structure. Group related items close, separate distinct sections with space.

### 5. Position
Users in Western cultures read top-to-bottom, left-to-right. Elements at the top-left receive attention first.

### 6. Typography
Different typefaces (a serif headline, a sans-serif body) create natural hierarchy without changing size.

## The F-Pattern and Z-Pattern

Eye-tracking studies show users scan pages in predictable patterns:

- **F-Pattern**: Content-heavy pages (blogs, news) — users read across the top, then scan down the left edge
- **Z-Pattern**: Sparse pages (landing pages) — users scan across top, diagonal, then across bottom

Design your most important elements along these paths.
